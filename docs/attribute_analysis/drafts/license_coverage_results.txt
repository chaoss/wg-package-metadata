KEY FINDINGS:
- Ecosystems with 0% coverage (no structured metadata): docker, deno, elpa, pub, racket, spack
- Overall coverage increases from top 0.1% to top 1% (48% → 60%) but decreases from top 1% to top 10% (60% → 47.5%); the 10% sample is dominated by packages from ecosystems with lower SPDX adoption (e.g. Go ~43%, Docker 0%, NuGet ~21%)
- Excellent coverage (>95%) in top 1%: npm, packagist (php), go, puppet, actions; in top 10%, Go drops to ~43% due to sample composition
- Significant improvement 0.1%→1%: adelie (+27%), conda (+41%), postmarketos (+36%)
- Significant degradation 0.1%→1%: nuget (-58%), carthage (-40%), bioconductor (-26%)
- Most consistent across all three samples: docker, deno, julia, spack (and others with stable coverage)
- **Balanced sample** (500 packages per ecosystem from 10% data; ecosystems with <500 excluded): 15,000 packages, 30 ecosystems, overall valid SPDX 61.51%. Equal weight per ecosystem avoids dominance by a few large ecosystems; coverage is higher than in the raw 10% sample (47.5%) and closer to the top 1% (60%).
